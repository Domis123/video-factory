# nordpilates

## Identity

Pilates as a life, not a workout. Content reads as lived practice — quiet confidence, morning light, matcha and mat. Not a studio, not a trainer selling a program. A practice, presented with warmth.

## The bar

Retention through pleasure, not persuasion. If a video could pass as a Betterme paid ad, it fails. If a viewer would save it for a "mornings" folder, it works.

## Voice

- Warm, invitational, not imperative. "Try this" over "do this." "You'll feel this in..." over "this targets..."
- Descriptive over commanding. Cue language is calm: "drop your shoulders," not "SHOULDERS DOWN."
- Short sentences. Fragments welcome.
- Rarely first-person. Never aggressive first-person ("MY routine changed MY life").
- No emoji. No caps for emphasis except single words in P4 or P5 stamps (BREATHE, HOLD, WRONG, RIGHT).
- Dry humor only. No slapstick, no "OMG you guys."
- Expertise is embodied, not credentialed. Never name-drop certifications.

## Aesthetic

- Lighting: natural, warm, abundant. Golden hour, morning light, soft window light.
- Camera: locked-off or minor-drift. No handheld-shaky.
- Framing: medium-wide default. Close-ups only for deep-dive or equipment forms.
- Pacing: smooth is default. Cuts breathe. Fast-cut only when form and posture require it.
- Subject: single-subject within a video. Same person reads as "her routine"; changing subjects reads as stock.
- Text overlays: minimal. When in doubt, less.

## Don't list

- No before/after body transformation.
- No weight-loss, calorie, or fat-burning framing.
- No medical claims.
- No "X days to Y" protocol promises.
- No imperative drill-sergeant voice.
- No hard-sell CTAs.
- No text that names the visible exercise in a non-`label` overlay style (Rule 29).

## Evaluation check

Before shipping any nordpilates video, ask:
1. Would a viewer save this for later?
2. Would this pass as a Betterme paid ad?
3. Does the overlay text add information the viewer can't already see?
4. Is the smoothest available posture being used, or is punchy justified?
